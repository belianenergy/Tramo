# Tramo Blueprint

Date: 2026-05-17  
Internal project: EnergyOS  
Public working brand: **Tramo**  
Mission: market-ready front/demo for launch conversations and lead capture.

## 1. CEO Summary

Tramo turns energy from an unexplained cost into an attribution and action layer for professional tourist-property portfolios.

The product connects reservations, CUPS/Datadis, invoices, tariffs and appliance rules. It shows which kWh belong to a stay, which kWh are non-operational, which contracted power/tariff decisions are wrong, and which owners need a clear monthly explanation.

The overnight goal is not a production SaaS. It is a credible, market-ready front that can capture leads and support sales demos with property managers.

## 2. Public Brand

### Name

**Tramo**

### Rationale

- Spanish energy bills are segmented by time periods and power periods.
- Reservations also happen in time windows.
- Operations are managed in intervals: pre-check-in, stay, checkout, idle.
- "Tramo" communicates precision, timing and control without sounding like generic green-energy SaaS.

### Taglines

Primary:

> Cada kWh, en su tramo correcto.

Landing H1:

> Convierte la energía de tu cartera turística en margen operativo.

Subcopy:

> Tramo cruza reservas, CUPS/Datadis, facturas y tarifas para que cada kWh tenga explicación, cada decisión tenga evidencia y cada propietario reciba un informe claro del impacto operativo sobre su factura.

### Logo Direction

No leaf. No lightning bolt as the main identity. No generic green eco mark.

Logo concept:

- A black monoline bracket/path that turns through three segments.
- One orange signal dot marking the active "tramo".
- Optional small tick marks suggesting tariff/reservation intervals.
- Wordmark: "Tramo" in a confident sans, lowercase or title case.

Visual feel: infrastructure diagram + editorial energy product.

Descriptor:

> Tramo — consumo atribuido por reserva y propietario

## 4. Audience

Primary:

- Spanish tourist-apartment/property managers with 10+ units.
- They manage owner expectations, reservations, check-in/out and operational incidents.
- They often do not control the energy stack as well as PMS/revenue/cleaning.

Secondary:

- Apart-hotels, villas and small hospitality operators.
- Energy consultants working with property portfolios.

## 5. Pain Map

### Pain 1: Consumption Outside Reservation

User sees high bills but cannot defend where consumption happened.

Visible data examples:

- "4.2 kWh sin reserva activa"
- "VGO-014 · checkout 11:00 · consumo 13:10-16:40"
- "Idle consumption: 18% of weekly kWh"
- "Accion: revisar termo y regla post-checkout"

### Pain 2: Tariff / Power Mismatch

Power or tariff is inherited, not managed.

Visible data examples:

- "P2 recomendada: 3.3 kW"
- "Actual: 4.6 / 4.6 kW"
- "Accion pendiente: revisar potencia valle"

### Pain 3: Owner Reporting

Owners see cost, not operational explanation.

Visible data examples:

- "Informe Abril · 412 kWh"
- "3 acciones de gestion"
- "42.50 EUR estimados por acciones defendibles"

## 6. Product Promise

Tramo helps managers:

1. Detect non-operational consumption.
2. Prepare operational rules for appliances around stays.
3. Review tariff and contracted power.
4. Produce owner-ready energy reports.
5. Identify portfolios where battery/arbitrage may be worth evaluating.

Important: battery/arbitrage is a premium module and qualification layer, not the core promise.

Core wedge:

> The product's core wedge is not generic energy management: it is explaining and acting on kWh by reservation window, idle period, tariff/power period and owner report. Every screen must reinforce this attribution layer.

## 7. Page Set

### 1. Public Landing

Purpose: convert qualified leads.

Required sections:

- Header with Tramo brand, nav and CTA.
- Hero with H1, subcopy, CTA, product panel.
- Pain triptych: consumption outside reservation, wrong tariff/power, owner reporting.
- System flow: PMS/reservations -> CUPS/Datadis/invoices -> decisions -> reporting.
- Modules grid.
- Product proof dashboard.
- Qualification CTA/form.

Primary CTA:

- "Diagnosticar mi cartera"

Secondary CTA:

- "Ver dashboard demo"

### 2. Product Dashboard

Purpose: show mission control for a portfolio.

Must include:

- Portfolio KPIs: kWh total, non-operational kWh, active alerts, estimated owner-ready actions.
- Chart: consumption by reservation state.
- Action queue.
- Property list summary.
- Date range controls.

### 3. Operations Queue

Purpose: make alerts actionable.

Must include:

- Filter tabs: all, outside reservation, tariff/power, appliance rules, owner report.
- Alert rows with property, timestamp, evidence, suggested action.
- Detail panel for selected alert.

### 4. Apartments / Portfolio

Purpose: compare units.

Must include:

- Table/cards with property code, owner, occupancy, kWh/stay, non-operational kWh, status.
- Filters by city, owner, PMS state, alert state.

### 5. Tariff & Power

Purpose: show structural optimization.

Must include:

- Actual vs recommended P1/P2.
- Current tariff vs suggested review.
- Savings shown as prudent estimate, not guarantee.
- Evidence and next action.

### 6. Owner Report

Purpose: prove trust and transparency.

Must include:

- Owner-facing monthly report.
- kWh, non-operational consumption, actions performed, estimated impact.
- Ready-to-send summary.

### 7. Leads / Pilot CRM

Purpose: internal qualification. This is an internal pilot workspace, not a customer-facing buyer page.

Must include:

- Leads table.
- Units, PMS, Datadis/factura availability, main pain, hardware interest.
- Status: new, qualified, call scheduled, pilot proposed.

### 8. Pilot Settings

Purpose: show setup realism.

Must include:

- Lightweight source status cards: PMS, Datadis/CUPS, invoices/CSV, appliance rules.
- Thresholds and notification rules.
- Pilot scope selector.

## 11. Acceptance Criteria

Within 10 seconds, a qualified visitor understands:

- This is for tourist-property managers.
- It connects reservation data and energy data.
- It controls appliances and detects waste.
- It helps review tariff/power.
- It generates owner-ready explanations.
- The CTA is to request a portfolio diagnosis/pilot.

Visual QA:

- Looks like energy infrastructure software, not AI SaaS.
- Cream/ink/orange identity is dominant.
- Product panels look real.
- Mobile header does not overlap.
- Text does not overflow buttons/cards.
- No decorative orbs, heavy gradients or generic phone mockups.

Technical QA:

- Next build passes.
- TypeScript passes.
- Landing and app pages render.
- Lead form persists through existing API or clearly mocked local route.
- Playwright desktop/mobile screenshots pass.
- Default lead form state submits without API enum mismatch.
- No horizontal scroll at 375px or 390px.
- Privacy/legal helper copy is visible near the form.
